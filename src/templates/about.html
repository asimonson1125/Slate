About Slate

Slate is an event coordination tool that combines the calendars of participants to automatically generate a table representing when the most participants can attend.

Although not initially inspired by the Where2Meet site, Slate replicates many aspects of Where2Meet and expands upon it via automated data collection and more flexible use cases.

Slate is the 2021-22 major project by Andrew Simonson with the intent to improve it and introduce analytics in the 2022-23 version. Bear with me on that.

Usage:

Every participant has three attributes, a URL, Name, and Score.

Slate is spreadsheet friendly! If you have a list of URLS, names, or scores, paste them in divided by newlines and the URLs will automatically overflow into new participant inputs.

URL

URLS are references to ics (iCalendar files) online. Most digital calendars use (or can export) ics calendars.

Google Calendars: Open calendar settings. Under 'Integrate Calendar', copy 'Public address in iCal format'. Depending on preferences, your calendar might need to be made public to work.

iCloud Calendars (iOS): in 'Edit Calendar', turn on 'Public Calendar' and share link.

Name

Optional input, names the participant. Defaults to 'Calendar ##'

Score

Scores determine the value of a participant's attendance. The higher the score, the worse it is when they cannot attend.

Scores of -1 indicate a mandatory attendance (aka the key speaker cannot make it or the room is not available) and such timeslots will be greyed out.
