About Slate

Slate will never reveal users' calendars or event details to users.

Slate is an event coordination tool that combines the calendars of participants to automatically generate a table representing when the most participants can attend.

Although not initially inspired by the Where2Meet site, Slate replicates many aspects of Where2Meet and expands upon it via automated long-term data collection and more flexible use cases.

The initial version of Slate is the 2021-22 major project by Andrew Simonson.

In Slate 2.0 the project (now 2022-23 major project, also Andrew Simonson) gained a much improved UX, large performance improvements, Computer Science House service integration, and basic analytics. This iteration is the first I consider publishable (delivers on all objectives and is user-friendly)

The source code for Slate and its further development can be found on Github.



Usage:

The example usage (in nav bar) shows a sample output. No work required :)

Every participant has three attributes, a URL, Name, and Score.

Slate is spreadsheet friendly! If you have a list of URLS, names, or scores, paste them in divided by newlines and the URLs will automatically overflow into new participant inputs.

URL

URLS are references to ics (iCalendar files) online. Most digital calendars use (or can export) ics calendars.

Google Calendar: Open calendar settings. Under 'Integrate Calendar', copy 'Public address in iCal format'. Depending on preferences, your calendar might need to be made public.

iCloud Calendars (iOS): in 'Edit Calendar', turn on 'Public Calendar' and share link.

Name

Optional input, names the participant. Defaults to 'Unnamed ##'

Score

Scores determine the value of a participant's attendance. The higher the score, the worse it is when they cannot attend.

Scores of -1 indicate a mandatory attendance (aka the key speaker cannot make it or the room is not available) and such timeslots will be greyed out.
